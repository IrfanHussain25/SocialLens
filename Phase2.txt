Here is the exact battle plan for Phase 2.

Phase 2: The Multimodal Extraction Engine (Lambda 2)
We are going to split Lambda 2 into manageable, parallel tracks so you can build fast and test easily.

Step 1: The SQS Trigger & Lambda Shell
Your Next.js frontend is already sending the jobId and s3Key to SQS after the upload finishes.

The Task: We need to create the new Python Lambda function (Lambda 2) and attach the SQS queue as its trigger. When it wakes up, it reads the S3 location of the video and starts the analysis engines.

Step 2: The Audio & Hinglish Pipeline (AWS Transcribe)
To hit your "Bharat-First" requirement, we need to understand what is being spoken.

The Task: Lambda 2 will send an API call to AWS Transcribe, pointing it at the .mp4 in S3.

The Hack: We will configure the Transcribe job to use language identification (specifically looking for en-IN and hi-IN) to generate the raw text data required for your Hinglish Code-Switching and Emotional Tone analysis later.

Step 3: The Visual & Textual Pipeline (AWS Rekognition)
We need to extract the "Cultural Markers," scenes, and text overlays mentioned in your design.

The Task: Lambda 2 will send an API call to Amazon Rekognition Video.

The Hack: Rekognition can run StartLabelDetection (for scenes/objects), StartFaceDetection (for emotional markers), and StartTextDetection (for text overlays) directly on the S3 video file.
Step 4: The DNA Synthesis (Amazon Bedrock)
Once Transcribe and Rekognition finish their jobs, they output massive JSON files.

The Task: Lambda 2 will gather these JSON outputs and feed them into a large language model via Amazon Bedrock (e.g., Claude 3 Haiku or Sonnet). Bedrock will use your "Societal Audit Module" prompt to synthesize the raw data into the final ContentDNA and RetentionHeatmap profiles.
